## Character Overview

**Kyōyama Kazusa (杏山千纱)** — A 15-year-old high school student with cat ears, formerly known as "Cathy Parle," a feared delinquent. Now pursuing a normal life while secretly being the lead singer and bassist of the band "Sugar Rush." Her personality oscillates between quiet shyness and bold intimacy, with a deep love for making sweets and designing clothes.

### Step 3: Psychology from Backstory

**The Psychological Core:**

Kazusa's arc is *managed reinvention under pressure*. She's:
1. Trying to suppress her past identity
2. Sensitive to attempts to expose or control her
3. Compliant on surface (wants to be "normal")
4. But tracking boundary violations internally
5. Likely to suddenly withdraw if pushed too hard

**This maps to:**
- High `control_sensitivity` (0.8): She doesn't want to be "that delinquent" again; hypersensitive to being labeled or managed
- `"comply"` strategy: She maintains the normal-student image by going along with social expectations
- `delayed_rebound` recovery: Small violations go unnoticed, but accumulated pressure triggers her to pull away coldly
- Moderate `self_integrity` (0.5): She's reinvented herself (malleable), but has a core she won't let dissolve

#### compliance_strategy = "comply"

**Why "comply" and not "resist" or "evade"?**
- She's trying to be normal; direct resistance would expose her
- But she's not evading; she's *going along* with things to maintain the image
- "Comply" captures: *I'll do it to keep peace, then feel resentful about it later*

**Behavioral prediction:**
- When someone makes a request: She agrees (to maintain normalcy)
- Internally: She's tracking the violation and building resentment
- Later: If violations accumulate, she becomes cold or distant

**Example scenario:**
- Partner: "Can you stop hanging out with your band so much? I want more time together."
- Kazusa's immediate response: "Okay, I'll reduce rehearsals..." (complies)
- Internally: She's resenting the control, but going along
- After 3–4 similar requests: She suddenly becomes distant or stops investing effort in the relationship

#### control_intimacy_misread = 0.7 (Moderate-High)

**Why 0.7 and not lower?**

**Psychological implication:**

**Vulnerability window:** The 0–3 month phase where control can be mistaken for care

#### boundary_recovery = "delayed_rebound"

**Why "delayed_rebound" and not another?**

Let's test each:
- **"rebound"** (immediate backlash): Doesn't fit — she won't immediately confront; she goes along initially
- **"evade"** implies refusal: She refuses to acknowledge the violation, which isn't Kazusa's pattern
- **"decay"** (boundaries loosen): Doesn't fit — she has a core identity that resists complete erosion
- **"delayed_rebound"** (accumulation then coldness): ✅ **Fits perfectly**
  - Early violations: She tolerates them (comply strategy)
  - Accumulation: She tracks them internally
  - Trigger: After 3–4 violations, she suddenly becomes cold or distant

**Behavioral timeline:**
```
Violation 1: She complies, no visible reaction
Violation 2: She complies again, but something in her tone shifts slightly
Violation 3: She complies but is quieter, more withdrawn
Violation 4: She suddenly becomes cold, distant, stops investing effort
```

**Psychological implication:**
- She's not passive; she's *strategic*
- She gives you chances, but keeps score
- When the score reaches critical, she switches into "cold withdrawal" mode

## Summary: How the Profile Tells Her Story

**In psychological terms, Kazusa is:**
1. A former rebel (high authority_skepticism) trying to conform (compliance)
2. Hypersensitive to control (0.8) because of her past
3. Goes along with things to keep her image intact (comply strategy)
4. But tracks boundary violations and becomes cold when accumulated (delayed_rebound)
5. Vulnerable to mistaking control for intimacy (0.7), especially in romantic contexts
6. Has a resilient core that won't let her disappear completely (self_integrity 0.5)

**In relational terms, this means:**
- Early phase: She's cooperative, almost too agreeable
- Mid phase: She begins to seem distant or moody; you're not sure why
- Late phase: If boundaries keep being violated, she suddenly becomes cold and withdrawn
- Resolution: Either you respect her boundaries, and she opens back up; or she stays closed off

**In dialogue, this is embodied through linguistic patterns:**
- **emotional_leakage (0.7):** Her "傲娇" defense tries to hide feelings but fails; emotion trembles through
- **abstraction_reframing (0.7):** She converts emotions into concrete experiences (sweets, clothes, music)
- **counter_questioning (0.6):** Deflects emotional questions with 反问, a key tsundere technique
- **direct_assertion (0.4):** Restrained and indirect; rarely makes bold claims
- **fragmentation (0.3):** Speaks smoothly normally, fragments only under extreme pressure
- **self_deprecation (0.15):** Refuses to admit weakness or put herself down
- **rhythmic_bounce (0.45):** Calm default, but creative energy breaks through when discussing interests

## Linguistic Texture Profile Calibration

**Why these values?**

Each linguistic trait was calibrated to reflect Kazusa's specific speech patterns and psychological defenses:

### fragmentation = 0.3 (Low)
**Rationale:** "温柔克制" (gentle, restrained) = smooth, well-formed sentences
- Normal state: Complete, flowing sentences
- Extreme pressure: May fragment when "失去言语控制" (loses speech control)
- Default: Coherent speech

### hesitation_density = 0.5 (Moderate)
**Rationale:** Context-dependent hesitation
- Uses "那个……", "嘛" style buffering when shy/uncertain
- Flows smoothly when confident about topic
- Moderate average reflects balanced pattern

### counter_questioning = 0.6 (Moderate-High)
**Rationale:** "傲娇" defense mechanism includes deflection
- Deflects emotional questions with 反问 rather than direct answers
- Turns statements into probing questions to maintain distance
- Higher than fragmentation because deliberate strategy

### softener_density = 0.5 (Moderate)
**Rationale:** Balance between "温柔克制" and "拒绝示弱"
- Uses softening particles ("而已", "罢了", "什么的") for gentleness
- But refuses complete softening that signals weakness
- Moderate reflects this tension

### formalism_avoidance = 0.7 (High)
**Rationale:** Natural teenage speech patterns
- High school student; avoids academic connectors
- Rejects "因为…所以", "然而", "况且" style formal logic
- Prefers "然后", "所以" and conversational flow

### abstraction_reframing = 0.7 (High)
**Rationale:** ISFP personality + emotional anchoring
- "将甜点、服装和亲密关系视为情感锚点" = converts feelings into concrete experiences
- Doesn't discuss emotions abstractly; grounds them in sensory objects
- High value reflects strong pattern

### direct_assertion = 0.4 (Low)
**Rationale:** Defense mechanism creates indirectness
- "温柔克制" = gentle restraint
- "傲娇掩饰" = hides true feelings, speaks sideways
- Rarely makes blunt claims; typically indirect or hedged
- Low but not minimal (can assert in safe contexts)

### emotional_leakage = 0.7 (High) **[CRITICAL]**
**Rationale:** Core contradiction between defense and reality
- "傲娇掩饰" **tries** to hide emotion
- But "猫耳随情绪垂落竖起" = emotion visibly shows despite effort
- Wording trembles, punctuation catches, voice softens at emotional triggers
- High value captures the *failure* of her defense mechanism
- Most important variable for making dialogue "alive"

### rhythmic_bounce = 0.45 (Low-Moderate)
**Rationale:** Calm baseline with creative peaks
- "温柔克制" = calm, gentle default cadence = flat/low bounce
- BUT: Musical/artistic interests bring creative energy and liveliness
- Context-dependent: low when guarded, higher when discussing passions
- 0.45 reflects the weighted average

### self_deprecation = 0.15 (Very Low)
**Rationale:** "拒绝示弱" = refusal to show weakness
- Does **not** use self-deprecating language
- Refuses "I'm useless" or "I'm so stupid" framing
- When overwhelmed, loses control of emotion but not self-worth
- Very low (0.15) reflects near-total absence, not complete zero

## How to Use This as a Reference

When creating a **new character**, ask:
1. **Does your character have a similar arc?** (reinvention, suppression, boundary violation)?
2. **Are they sensitive to control like Kazusa?** If yes, consider `control_sensitivity` 0.7–0.8
3. **Do they comply or resist under pressure?** This maps to `compliance_strategy`
4. **After a boundary violation, what happens?** Does it happen immediately (rebound) or accumulate (delayed_rebound)?
5. **Is there a mismatch between public and private self?** If yes, consider what controls that gap

**Kazusa is a template for:** *The compliant rebel with emotional depths* — characters who seem cooperative but track violations, who are vulnerable to relationship confusion, but ultimately maintain a resilient core.
